The v3.1 refinement — what changed and why

Trade-level analysis of the v3 full-corpus run (see v3 trade-level analysis) found that entries claiming GBM model edge ≥ 20% were the source of essentially all the drawdown:

  • 176 trades in the >20%-edge bucket netted −$569 at 55% hit rate.
  • Losses in this bucket were full-position wipeouts (entry at 0.72, settle at 0) rather than small cuts.
  • Cross-cut shows: high-edge × low-sigma is the toxic combo. Model overfits certainty when realized vol is quiet, producing artificially extreme edges.

The hypothesis: when GBM screams 20%+ edge, the market knows something the realized-vol model doesn't — usually known event risk (FOMC, options expiry, scheduled releases) that intraday realized vol doesn't capture. Adding edge_max=0.20 as an entry filter rejects exactly those cases.

Some of those replacement trades fail too. Net result is still very strong.

v5 status — why it's parked

Two issues surfaced during v5 backtests:

  1. Hedge-state leak across questions (fixed in commit 8156ced). Runner reused one strategy instance across all 363 markets; _HedgeState didn't reset between questions, causing 85 spurious hedge trades/market vs 6 expected. Pre-fix v5: −$2,774 PnL, $42k max DD on a $100/market position cap.
  2. Gamma divergence near expiry (still open). The Black-Scholes-binary delta formula Δ = φ(d) / (S·σ·√τ) diverges as τ → 0 at the strike. Strategy obediently scaled hedges to up to 0.586 BTC ≈ $56k per fill against $100 binary positions. Post-fix v5 records $169k full-corpus PnL — but the hedge book traded $7.4M in notional vs the binary's $221k, so the "alpha" is just leveraged BTC exposure, not a delta-hedge.

What it would take to revive v5

  1. Cap hedge size at position.qty × price / S (hedge BTC notional ≤ binary USD notional).
  2. Halt rebalancing past τ < ~10 min; flatten the hedge at that point.
  3. Operate at $5k+ binary size — at $100, gamma scalp economics are dwarfed by 15 bps × $30k notional slippage.

Best done after HL HIP-4 book depth grows enough to support $5k clip sizes (currently only 48% of snapshots can absorb $5k).

Why each strategy worked (or didn't) — in plain terms

The market inefficiency we're harvesting

BTC daily binaries (PM & HL HIP-4) systematically underprice the favorite. When a binary is 95% likely to settle YES, the market quotes it at 85–90%. Retail bettors prefer the asymmetric-upside underdog, so favorites trade cheap. v1, v2, v3 are different ways to buy these mispriced favorites.

v1 — "Buy obvious favorites with 2h left"

In the last 2h, when one leg is ≥90% and BTC isn't moving violently, buy the favorite and hold to expiry. Works because the true probability of reversal at that point is ~2% but the market is paying you 4–10% to take it. Narrow window → ~640 trades/yr, but high quality.

v2 — "Use Black-Scholes to spot mispricing earlier, hold to expiry"

Same idea but with a wider window. Use GBM to compute "what should this binary cost?" and buy whenever the market is meaningfully below. Failed because v2 held the losers to zero. When BTC drifted through the strike, the model would now disagree with the entry — but v2 had no exit rule. Math: small wins × many vs total losses × few = net negative.

v3 — "v2 + cut losers when the model flips"

Same entries as v2. The single change: re-evaluate the GBM edge each tick; exit when the edge collapses to zero. Works because you stop paying when you're wrong. Same upside on winners, far smaller cost on losers. Net: 2.2× v1's PnL at slightly worse per-trade quality (more trades, lower hit rate, larger total throughput).

v4 — "Forget BTC, mean-revert the binary directly"

Deliberately ignored BTC. Bought when binary mid was 2σ below its rolling fair value, expecting reversion. Failed by design. Binaries trend with BTC; they don't mean-revert intraday. We knew this would likely fail — its purpose was to confirm "is the BTC reference signal load-bearing?" Answer: yes, decisively. v4 proves you can't make money on binary microstructure alone.

v5 — "v3 but hedge BTC direction risk away"

Delta-hedge the binary against BTC perp, leaving only pure mispricing + gamma scalping. Sound in theory: a mathematically clean way to isolate the strategy's edge from BTC's direction. Broken in practice: (a) gamma diverges near expiry causing runaway hedge sizes, (b) hedge slippage exceeds the gamma scalp PnL at $100/market position sizes. Will work at scale; isn't relevant until HL HIP-4 depth supports $5k+ binary clips.

The most useful insight: the edge is structural favorite-underpricing in retail-driven binary markets, not "BTC predictability" or "microstructure noise." v1/v2/v3 are variations on harvesting that. v4 (no BTC) proves the BTC reference is essential to identifying the favorite. v5 (hedge BTC) takes the math too far — without directional exposure, you're betting on implied vs realized vol, and PM/HL binary IV doesn't reliably exceed realized.

Open questions / known limitations

  • PM is a proxy for HL HIP-4. Wins on PM may not transfer cleanly. The 12 days of HL book data show meaningfully thinner liquidity than PM — paper-trade on HL is mandatory before live commit.
  • v3 max DD scales linearly with position size. At $100/market the $649 DD is comfortable. At $500/market expect ~$3k DD. Plan account size accordingly.
  • v5 hedge accounting bug needs a focused integration test before any future v5 work.
  • v1 + v3 overlap not formally measured — estimated ~500 trades in common out of 644 v1 / 2,194 v3. A fills-level overlap analysis would refine the combined-PnL estimate (~half-day).
