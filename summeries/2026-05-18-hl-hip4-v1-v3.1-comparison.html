HL fee model (post-task feedback)

HL HIP-4 fees are asymmetric: buys = 0 bps; sells = 7 bps taker / 4 bps maker. The backtests above used --fee-taker 0.0 (symmetric), which understates v3.1's true cost when its exit is a marketable sell. v1 is unaffected because its "sells" are synthetic settlement bookings at price = 1.0 (the contract pays out — no exchange order).

Bucket: three backtester bugs masked real performance

The first bucket run returned 0 trades / 7,150 no_book diagnostics. The second run reported −$331. Investigation surfaced three real backtester bugs in the bucket path; after all three are fixed v3.1 bucket is +$29.93 (default) / +$38.80 (realistic) at 80% hit, and v1 bucket clears +$20.72 (70% hit, $0 DD). The bugs:

#BugWhereEffectFix
1 kv dropped on QuestionView hftbt_runner.run_one_question When strike > 0 (always true on HL because runner derives it from first ref event), the runner built QuestionView via build_question_view(...) with kv=(). Strategy reads priceThresholds from kv to compute per-leg winning regions — empty kv → (None, None) region for every bucket leg → no_book every tick → 0 trades. Only use the strike-override path for q.klass == "priceBinary"; route bucket markets through data_source.question_view(...) which carries the real kv.
2 Exit-guard missed ask=None theta_harvester._evaluate_exits Guard checked held.bid_px is None only. When the held bucket leg loses its ask side (common late in HL HIP-4 bucket markets), the edge calc dereferenced None and the runner crashed mid-question. Add held.ask_px is None to the guard; hold until the ask reappears.
3 Settlement payoff hard-coded to binary semantics hftbt_runner._settle_px_for_outcome The critical one. Settled at 1.0 only if pos.symbol == leg_symbols[0] (YES) or leg_symbols[1] (NO). For buckets the held leg is somewhere in the middle of the leg list (e.g. #230 at idx 2), so every bucket position settled at 0.0 regardless of which bucket actually won. Q3 example: BTC ended at 80,814 (between thresholds 79,043 and 82,270), #230 bid was 0.995 at expiry (clear winner), v1 entered #230 at 0.988 → paid out 0 → reported as −$98 loss when reality was +$1.20. Add leg_payoff(q, leg_symbol) to HLHip4DataSource: for buckets, compute the winning outcome_pos from BTC final mark + priceThresholds, pay 1.0 to the YES leg of the winning bucket and to NO legs of every non-winning named bucket, 0.0 otherwise. Runner duck-types this method.

Also dropped in this pass: v3.1's bucket entry now restricts to YES legs only (mirrors v1 — line 531 of late_resolution.py). The NO of bucket k is structurally redundant with the union of YES legs of all other buckets; on a small HL bucket corpus this redundancy produced contradictory bets where the strategy entered YES of one bucket and NO of an adjacent bucket at different ticks, sometimes leading to multi-leg simultaneous losses.

Caveats

  • Sample size: 11 daily binary expiries vs 363 on PM (~3% the sample). Any single market swings PnL by $20–$100. Treat all numbers as point estimates with $±30 (v1) and $±50 (v3.1) per-market σ. The Sharpe values are not interpretable below ~100 trades.
  • Depth limitation: HL HIP-4 spreads p50 ≈ 173 bps, p75 ≈ 1,265 bps — far wider than PM. The realistic-cost run partially captures this but cannot reproduce intermittent liquidity gaps that would actually halt orders live.
  • Geographic difference: HL retail flow is concentrated in EMEA/APAC perp users speculating on binaries adjacent to BTC perp; PM is US prediction-market users. This is the most likely source of any persistent edge difference and we can't validate it from 12 days.
  • Fee model: All runs used symmetric fee_taker=0; HL is asymmetric (0 / 7 bps). Net impact ~$1 on this window.
  • Bucket result: all three backtester bugs in the bucket path were latent. Once the runner pays out correctly per-leg, both v1 and v3.1 clear positive on the 10-market sample. Even so, 10 expiries is a small N — extrapolating: v3.1 ≈ $3/market on HL bucket vs $5.35/market on PM binary. Worth recording another 30+ bucket expiries before any size scaling.

Recommendation

  1. v3.1 binary, default cost: Ship to live shadow against v1 for another ~30 expiries. Decision threshold: v3.1 net PnL > v1 net PnL at the 60% confidence level (~50 markets cumulative).
  2. v3.1 binary, realistic cost: Sized at $100/position the strategy stays comfortably profitable after asymmetric HL fees; no config change needed.
  3. v3.1 bucket: Proceed to live shadow alongside v1 bucket. Strategy + backtester are aligned now (three fixes shipped) and both variants are net positive at 80% hit on the 10-market sample. Default-cost variant suffices; the realistic-cost filter actually trades slightly tighter and earns more. Re-evaluate after another 30+ bucket expiries accumulate to confirm the small-sample edge.
  4. v1: Stays live; production config validated on HL HIP-4 without changes.
