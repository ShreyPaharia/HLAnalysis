HL fee model (post-task feedback)

HL HIP-4 fees are asymmetric: buys = 0 bps; sells = 7 bps taker / 4 bps maker. The backtests above used --fee-taker 0.0 (symmetric), which understates v3.1's true cost when its exit is a marketable sell. v1 is unaffected because its "sells" are synthetic settlement bookings at price = 1.0 (the contract pays out — no exchange order).

Bucket: loses money, two wiring fixes shipped

The first bucket run returned 0 trades / 7,150 no_book diagnostics. Two bugs in the bucket path were found and fixed before the numbers above were generated:

BugWhereEffectFix
kv dropped on QuestionView hftbt_runner.run_one_question When strike > 0 (always true on HL because runner derives it from first ref event), the runner built QuestionView via build_question_view(...) with kv=(). Strategy reads priceThresholds from kv to compute per-leg winning regions — empty kv → (None, None) region for every bucket leg → no_book. Only use the strike-override path for q.klass == "priceBinary"; route bucket markets through data_source.question_view(...) which carries the real kv.
Exit-guard missed ask=None theta_harvester._evaluate_exits line 372 Guard checked held.bid_px is None only. When the held bucket leg loses its ask side (common late in HL HIP-4 bucket markets), line 402's edge calc dereferenced None and the runner crashed mid-question. Add held.ask_px is None to the guard; hold (no edge-exit decision) until the ask reappears.

After both fixes the bucket strategy enters real trades and exercises the full v3.1 logic. The results are negative on this sample:

  • Default cost: 44 trades, −$331.63, hit 50%, max DD $331.63. Each loss is the full $100 max position (the strategy enters near-favorites that flip).
  • Realistic cost: 48 trades, −$223.65, hit 60%, max DD $237.71. The wider half_spread_assumption acts as an extra edge filter and rejects the most marginal trades, which is why the realistic-cost variant loses less.
The bucket params inherited from binary do not transfer. Binary v3.1 was tuned on the PM Up/Down corpus where the favorite-vs-fade distribution is well-behaved. On HL HIP-4 buckets, edge-bucket NO legs trade near 1.0 most of the time and the rare flip costs the full $100 — exactly the toxic-edge bucket that edge_max=0.20 was designed to filter, but bucket edges live in a different range. A dedicated bucket walk-forward (likely tighter edge_max, asymmetric YES-vs-NO entry, and a true favorite_threshold at 0.9+) is needed before any bucket exposure goes live.

Caveats

  • Sample size: 11 daily binary expiries vs 363 on PM (~3% the sample). Any single market swings PnL by $20–$100. Treat all numbers as point estimates with $±30 (v1) and $±50 (v3.1) per-market σ. The Sharpe values are not interpretable below ~100 trades.
  • Depth limitation: HL HIP-4 spreads p50 ≈ 173 bps, p75 ≈ 1,265 bps — far wider than PM. The realistic-cost run partially captures this but cannot reproduce intermittent liquidity gaps that would actually halt orders live.
  • Geographic difference: HL retail flow is concentrated in EMEA/APAC perp users speculating on binaries adjacent to BTC perp; PM is US prediction-market users. This is the most likely source of any persistent edge difference and we can't validate it from 12 days.
  • Fee model: All runs used symmetric fee_taker=0; HL is asymmetric (0 / 7 bps). Net impact ~$1 on this window.
  • Bucket result: after two wiring fixes the strategy now trades, but with binary-tuned params the bucket PnL is negative on 10 markets. Bucket needs its own tuning corpus (we have only 10 expiries so far — too few). Likely needs tighter edge_max and per-position $-cap on edge-bucket NO legs.

Recommendation

  1. v3.1 binary, default cost: Ship to live shadow against v1 for another ~30 expiries. Decision threshold: v3.1 net PnL > v1 net PnL at the 60% confidence level (~50 markets cumulative).
  2. v3.1 binary, realistic cost: Sized at $100/position the strategy stays comfortably profitable after asymmetric HL fees; no config change needed.
  3. v3.1 bucket: Do not deploy. Strategy is wired correctly now (two fixes shipped) but the binary-tuned params lose $33/market on this 10-market sample. Bucket needs its own walk-forward calibration: at minimum (a) a tighter edge_max for edge-bucket NO legs, (b) per-leg position size shaped by distance from threshold, (c) a real HL bucket holdout with 30+ expiries before any live exposure.
  4. v1: Stays live; production config validated on HL HIP-4 without changes.
