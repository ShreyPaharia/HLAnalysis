The model's confidence is wrong when the edge is "too big." When GBM says p_model = 0.99 but the market quotes 0.65, the market knows something — usually event risk (news, FOMC, options expiry) that the realized-vol model doesn't see. Hit rate stays at 55%, but losers are full-position wipeouts while winners only collect 25-35 cents of price move.

v3.1 — the live filter (implemented and re-run)

After the counterfactual analysis surfaced the toxic-edge bucket, edge_max: Optional[float] was added to ThetaHarvesterConfig. When set, entries with max(edge_yes, edge_no) ≥ edge_max emit a HOLD with diagnostic edge_too_extreme. Production config sets edge_max=0.20; default None preserves v3 baseline behaviour for existing tuning grids.

Recommended next iterations of v3

  1. Add upper-edge cap: entry_edge_max = 0.20. Single highest-leverage change. Expected effect: PnL $1,561 → $2,130, max DD $652 → $103, Sharpe ~2.1 → ~4.5. Half-day of work + a re-tune to confirm.
  2. Investigate the 9th-10th losers. The 89.5%-entry / high-sigma full-loss is a different beast than the high-edge tail. May want a per-binary-price stop-loss for setups with sigma > 1.0.
  3. Test edge-cap × sigma-floor combo. Drop both edge ≥ 0.20 AND sigma < 0.30 entries. The cross-cut suggests this could push hit rate up further; might also drop trade count too aggressively to be worth it.
  4. Run the same analysis on live HL HIP-4 data after 30 days. Confirm the edge-tail toxicity pattern holds — it's a PM-derived hypothesis until HL data validates.

Key takeaways for production

  • v3's exit mechanism is sound — 88% of losers cut at <$20, median loser only −$2.50. The MTM design works as intended.
  • The drawdown comes from a small, identifiable tail. 33 full-position wipeouts (3% of trades) drive most of the $652 DD. Most of those are in the >20%-edge bucket.
  • "Too much edge" is a red flag, not a green light. When the model claims to see massive mispricing, distrust it — the market usually knows something the realized-vol GBM model doesn't.
  • Low vol regime is dangerous for v3. When sigma < 30%, the model produces narrow distributions that yield artificially extreme edges. Either filter low-vol entries or apply a tighter edge cap when vol is low.
  • v3 is genuinely better than v2 for the right reason. v2 lost because it held losers to zero. v3 cuts them — but cuts them at the right tail (median loser −$2.50, not −$50). The mechanism is doing what we designed it to do.
