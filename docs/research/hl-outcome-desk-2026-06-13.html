HL Outcome-Market Desk — Findings & Strategy Recommendations

Desk-manager analysis of 36 days (2026-05-06 → 2026-06-10) of granular Hyperliquid data: BTC perp + daily BTC binary & bucket outcome markets. All edge claims split-half validated. Generated 2026-06-13.

Live progress log: hl-outcome-desk-progress.html · Reusable toolkit: hlanalysis/research/ · Spec: SPEC

Bottom line. The HL daily-BTC outcome markets contain a single, robust, model-free edge: a favourite-longshot bias — strong favorites (binary mid 0.80–0.95) are systematically underpriced by ~6–9pp, driven by the market overpricing volatility (+14% ann vs realized, sign-stable). It survives adverse selection and fees (HL exchange fee = 0). But it is not deployable as a standalone single-name BTC-binary bet: at ~1 qualifying favorite per day the strategy is capacity-starved (n=28 trades in 36 days) and dominated by a fat left tail (one losing favorite = −87pp), so out-of-sample it went negative on the 8-trade holdout. The edge becomes a desk only through breadth — running favorite-capture across many markets simultaneously (ETH binaries, bucket bands, and the already-recorded Polymarket multi-strike/multi-asset series). Market-making is independently +EV on paper (makers keep ~80% of a 128bp spread net of adverse selection). The binary tracks perp sub-second (1–2s half-life), so a passive MM must cancel/replace in <~1s — and the live engine already runs event-driven at ~5 Hz (scan_min_interval_seconds 0.2), so MM is viable on current infra pending a one-time end-to-end cancel-replace latency check, not blocked. Two candidates (static Yes+No arb, perp-momentum taking) were killed on the evidence.

1 · Strategy scorecard & recommendations

① DEPLOY (with work): Favorite-capture across breadth  conditional pass
The FLB edge is real (+6.15pp mean, split-half +6.9/+5.4pp). To make it deployable, extend the existing v1/v31 favorite-buying to many markets at once so the law of large numbers tames the single-settlement tail. Priority order by data already in hand: HL bucket bands → HL ETH binaries (if listed) → PM multi-strike BTC/ETH/SOL/XRP series (recorded since 2026-06-06). Keep the min_safety_d/stop_loss safety gates — they cost trade count, not edge, and the tail risk is exactly what they exist for. Size per-market small ($50–200, capacity-bound) and diversify across ≥10–20 concurrent markets. Acceptance before live: re-run the clean-FLB backtest on the multi-market corpus and clear Sharpe≥1 OOS at the portfolio level (the single BTC binary cannot).
② VIABLE ON CURRENT ENGINE (pending latency check): Outcome-market making + perp hedge  +EV, measure latency first
Maker economics are positive (Card B: 0.95pp realized spread at 5m, ~80% of the quoted spread kept net of adverse selection, profitable on 55% of trades; books are WIDE_SPARSE — 128bp median spread, ~100% two-sided uptime). The HL-perp delta hedge is clean (hedge ratio ≈0.50, stable) and effectively unlimited in size. Latency requirement (Card C): perp→binary reprices with a 1–2s half-life, so a passive quote must be cancel/replaced in <~1s (ideally <500ms) or it gets picked off. The live engine already meets the scan side — it runs event-driven at scan_min_interval_seconds: 0.2 (~5 Hz in active markets, since 2026-06-05), well inside the repricing window. The one thing to confirm before quoting passively is the end-to-end cancel-replace ack RTT under load (scan cadence ≠ full round-trip; HL RTT ~100–500ms puts the total at ~300–700ms — likely fine, at the edge). Recommendation: finish the passive-fill sim (strategy_mm.py) for a queue-position-honest return estimate, measure the live cancel-replace RTT, then pilot MM at small size — it is NOT infra-blocked.
③ DROPPED on evidence  killed
Static Yes+No arb — book is consistently ~90bp overround; 0 of 3.18M ticks offered a taker buy-arb (Card D). Perp-momentum taking on the binary — perp move predicts binary move but R²=0.08 and net edge −0.36pp, swamped by the binary half-spread (Card C). Neither warrants further work.

3 · The unifying thesis

Four cards independently point to one mechanism:

Market overprices volatility (F: +14%)  →  binary mids pulled toward 0.5  →  favorites underpriced (E: +18.8pp; D's GBM "rich" was a σ-horizon artifact)  →  buying favorites is +EV and not adversely selected (B).

The constraint everywhere is capacity / breadth (A: $107 TOB; only 1 BTC binary/day). The edge per market is small and the single-name tail is fat; the desk's job is to harvest it across as many uncorrelated-settlement markets as possible. This is consistent with the live desk being profitable-but-small and scaling by adding markets.
