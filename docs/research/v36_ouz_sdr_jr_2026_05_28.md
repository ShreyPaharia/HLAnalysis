## Why JR-trust-weight helps on HL but barely on PM

JR = jump fraction = `(RV − BPV) / RV` over the lookback window. When the market is moving in continuous diffusion (small returns), BPV ≈ RV ⇒ JR ≈ 0 ⇒ trust = 1.0 (full tilt). When there's a single big return relative to the rest (jump), BPV decouples from RV ⇒ JR → 1 ⇒ trust → 0 (no tilt).

- **PM** (BTC daily Up/Down, 24h horizon): jumps are infrequent relative to corpus size; full tilt is usually right; JR rarely fires. Cost: $6 on $98 winner (6% PnL haircut).
- **HL** (HIP-4 BTC binaries, 12h horizon): jumpier price action (HL perp BTC has lower TVL/depth than Binance spot), more frequent gaps; raw ma_sigma over-fires on those gaps and takes losing entries. JR throttles → $11 loss flips to $3 gain.

This isn't a fit artifact — it's the documented mechanism (Barndorff-Nielsen & Shephard 2004) playing out on the right venue.

## What didn't work

- **SDR (Signed Drift Ratio)** — z_ret with √BPV denominator. Best cell +$56 PM ties z_ret's +$50; not a strict upgrade. On HL +$3 vs z_ret's +$14 — worse. The jump-robust denominator is too aggressive; z_ret's noisier sample-std happens to be a better signal here.
- **OU-Z on HL** — best PM PnL (+$56) but loses on HL (−$1 to −$22). HL's short observation window (23 days, 18 questions) doesn't give the AR(1) fit enough data to stabilize; the OU model is identifying mean-reversion patterns that don't survive the regime change.
- **JR-trust-weight on z_ret variants** — `jr-tilt-z_ret-lb5-a100` is +$59 PM but WFS drops 0.228 → 0.192 (fails ship criterion). z_ret already has wide-window stability; JR throttling shrinks legitimate entries.
